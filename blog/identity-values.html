Identity, Values and Strengths

Describe your core personal values

While thinking of the desired superpower as a kid, all I wanted was to look at the world through someone else's eyes, relate to their feelings. I don't recall when I noticed that this superpower was real, and I was blessed to have it. Later on, I realized it's something adults call "empathy". I have been reflecting on empathy quite a lot during the past few years, since it seems to be the key to solving multiple problems these days. Focus and simplicity are the values I am mastering at the moment.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

I remember facing ethical dilemmas quite often during my first year of studying at the uni. If I said I never cheated during the exams it would be a lie. Analyzing my exam experience, I started to realize that cheating makes me feel absolutely exhausted and the perspective of getting a lower grade (my TRUE grade) started to seem way more pleasing than experiencing self-blame, shame and guilt. Besides, it was useless in terms of self-improvement and pushing my boundaries. Most importantly, I finally became fully aware of the fact that betraying the trust of the tutors and the belief in my own abilities was not ethically correct and unfair. Thus, I managed to quit cheating, and I am pleased I was able to take this step.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I grew up in the Far East of Russia. Integrity, commitment, quality orientation are some of the values I observed in people around me. My parents tried to avoid telling me what was right or wrong - they have always served as an example of honesty and balance to me.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

After finding my first job as a teacher in Russia, I felt some tension working with a group of adults. Since I just graduated from the uni and didn't have any relevant teaching experience, my ability to fulfill this role was gloomily questioned by one of the students :) It took me about a week to resolve this issue. First, I did my best at identifying what the student's learning goal was, understanding his motives. It turned out he was pressed for time and stressed out due to this fact. When I had a clear picture of his needs, I adjusted his learning plan and created some extra learning material and personal learning tips for him. When a big goal was divided into a number of sub-goals it felt way easier for both of us to achieve it. How about adding a bit of inspiration? - I didn't miss this step! Quite quickly resistance changed to gratitude.
